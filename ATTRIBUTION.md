# Attribution

## Methods

PitForge implements well-established open-pit optimisation theory. The science is attributable to:

- **Lerchs, H. & Grossmann, I. F. (1965).** Optimum design of open-pit mines. *CIM Bulletin*, 58, 47–54., the
  ultimate-pit graph algorithm PitForge computes (the modern way).
- **Picard, J.-C. (1976).** Maximal closure of a graph and applications to combinatorial problems. *Management
  Science*, 22(11), 1268–1272., the max-closure → minimum-cut reduction PitForge uses.
- **Hochbaum, D. S. (2008).** The pseudoflow algorithm. *Operations Research*, 56(4), 992–1009., the modern exact
  UPL solver; PitForge’s min-cut is the same cut, computed with Dinic’s max-flow.
- **Dinic, E. A. (1970).** Algorithm for solution of a problem of maximum flow in networks. *Soviet Math. Doklady*,
  11, 1277–1280., the max-flow engine.
- **Whittle, J. (1988).** Beyond optimization in open pit design., the revenue-factor nested-shell parameterisation.
- **Hustrulid, Kuchta & Martin (2013).** *Open Pit Mine Planning and Design* (3rd ed.). CRC Press., the reference text.

Full bibliography in `frontend/src/data/citations.ts` and the in-app Methodology page.

## Data

All deposits are **synthetic**, seeded grade fields generated by `frontend/src/opt/blockmodel.ts` (a geological
trend + a spatially-correlated noise field). No real drillhole or block-model data is used or redistributed. The
`CTRL` case is a closed-form analytic control.
